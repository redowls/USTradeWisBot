# Technical Design & Best Practices for an Intraday US Stock Trading Bot (Alpaca Paper Trading, Python, SQL Server, Telegram)

## TL;DR
- Build the bot as modular layers (data → signal engine → confidence/sizing → execution → DB logging → alerting → scheduler), start on Alpaca **paper trading** (`paper=True`, base URL `https://paper-api.alpaca.markets`), and use **bracket orders** to auto-attach stop-loss/take-profit. The single most important architectural decision is to treat the strategy as unproven: paper results overstate live performance, so log everything for later comparison.
- For signals, combine **swing-pivot/fractal support-resistance breakouts** (confirmed by candle close + volume + a percentage buffer + retest) with **triple-EMA alignment** (8>10>20 and 21>34>55 "stacking"), gate both behind a trend/regime filter, then fuse them into a **0–100 confidence score**; size positions with **ATR-based risk-per-trade capped at 1–2% of equity** and at most **quarter-to-half Kelly** — never full Kelly.
- Two material 2026 facts change the plan: (1) FINRA **eliminated the $25,000 Pattern Day Trader rule effective June 4, 2026**, and Alpaca is removing the PDT API fields by July 6, 2026 — build sizing on `buying_power`, not the deprecated PDT fields; (2) store **trades/signals/P&L/watchlist in SQL Server** but **never store API keys in the database** — use a `.env` file with python-dotenv, gitignored and chmod 600.

## Key Findings

### 1. Support & Resistance detection
- **Swing pivots / Williams fractals** are the workhorse method: a bar is a pivot high if its high exceeds the highs of *n* bars on each side (typical lookback 2–5 intraday). Bill Williams fractals use a fixed 5-bar pattern (middle bar highest high = bearish/resistance fractal; lowest low = bullish/support fractal). These confirm only after *n* right-side bars complete (lag = n bars), so they are confirmation tools, not predictors.
- **Programmatic toolset:** pandas/numpy for OHLCV, `scipy.signal.argrelextrema`/`find_peaks` for local extrema, rolling min/max for rolling support/resistance, and `AgglomerativeClustering` (scikit-learn) to merge nearby pivots into discrete price levels (the `support_resistance` and `pytrendline` libraries demonstrate both). Volume Profile adds **High-Volume Nodes (HVN)**, the **Point of Control (POC)** and **Value Area (70% of volume, VAH/VAL)** as the most statistically reliable S/R zones — HVN acts as resistance when price is below it, support when above.
- **Valid breakout definition (professional consensus):** (a) a **candle CLOSE** beyond the level, not an intrabar wick touch; (b) **volume surge** (commonly ≥120–150% of the 20-bar average); (c) a **percentage/ATR buffer** above the level to avoid noise; (d) a **prior-touch count** (a level tested 2+ times is more valid); and (e) ideally a **retest** that holds (S/R "flip"). Waiting for a retest reduces false entries but misses fast movers — an explicit tradeoff.
- **Over-extension / "good value" filters:** distance of price from the breakout level; **distance from a moving average measured in ATR multiples** (e.g., +2 ATR from the 50-SMA = healthy, +3 ATR = overextended, +7 ATR = profit-taking zone); **RSI** (>70 overbought, but in strong trends RSI can stay overbought — don't blindly counter-trade); and **aberrant range** (a daily range >175% of the 20-day ATR is "too far, too fast"). Rule: don't chase entries far from the mean — wait for value near dynamic support.

### 2. Triple moving average strategy
- **Alignment/"stacking" logic:** bullish when fast > medium > slow (8>10>20 for the short set; 21>34>55 for the long set — 21/34/55 are Fibonacci numbers commonly used in EMA ribbons). Bearish is the inverse. A discrete signal: +1 when fully stacked bullish, −1 when stacked bearish, 0 otherwise.
- **EMA vs SMA:** EMA is more responsive (less lag) and is preferred for intraday; SMA is smoother and favored for long-term. The classic golden cross (50>200) / death cross are slow, long-horizon signals; the triple-EMA approach is faster.
- **Signal strength scoring:** use **slope** (rising EMAs) and **separation** (the gap/fanning between EMAs) — widening separation with positive slope = strong trend; converging EMAs = weakening/chop. Convert to a 0–1 sub-score.
- **Critical weakness (contrarian):** raw MA crossovers **whipsaw badly in range-bound markets and lag**. CrossTrade reports a raw 9/21 EMA crossover on ES fires ~30–50 trades/quarter with 60–65% losers and ~1.0 profit factor (break-even) without a filter — "the filters are the strategy; the crossover is just the trigger." Their guidance: **a regime filter (ADX > 20 or higher-timeframe trend alignment) matters far more than tuning the exact MA periods.** Tightly-spaced EMAs (your 8/10/20) whipsaw most on 5-minute charts.

### 3. Confidence scoring & position sizing
- **Composite confidence (0–100):** weighted sum of normalized sub-scores — e.g., breakout quality (close beyond + buffer), volume confirmation, MA alignment/slope/separation, momentum (RSI/MACD), and proximity-to-value (penalize overextension). Confluence (multiple independent signals agreeing) is the core principle; weights should be **derived from theory, not over-optimized** (each tuned weight/threshold is a free parameter that inflates overfitting risk).
- **Position sizing methods:** fixed-fractional (risk a constant 1–2% of equity per trade; scales with account), volatility/ATR-based (size so a stop at N×ATR risks the fixed %), and Kelly. **Kelly is too aggressive in raw form** — full Kelly can produce 50–80% drawdowns and is hypersensitive to win-rate estimation error. Use **quarter- to half-Kelly** (half-Kelly captures ~75% of growth at ~50% of the volatility) layered **under a hard 1–2% risk cap**. Requires 50–100+ trades before Kelly inputs are statistically meaningful.
- **"More confidence = more money" done responsibly:** scale the risk fraction *within* the cap by confidence — e.g., risk % = min(cap, base% × confidence_scale) — but never let any single trade exceed the hard cap regardless of what the formula outputs, and account for **correlation** (multiple correlated longs = concentrated single-factor exposure).

### 4. Intraday risk management & day-trading rules
- **ATR stops/targets:** stop = entry − (ATR × multiplier); intraday traders typically use **0.6–1.0× ATR** (tighter), swing traders 1.5–3×. Take-profit via a risk-reward ratio (commonly 1:2). **Trailing stop** = highest-high-since-entry − (ATR × multiplier); it only moves up, locking gains. Use the ATR of the timeframe you trade.
- **PDT rule — MAJOR 2026 CHANGE:** FINRA's amended Rule 4210 **eliminated the $25,000 minimum equity requirement and the Pattern Day Trader designation entirely**. Per FINRA Regulatory Notice 26-10 (published April 20, 2026): *"The effective date of the amendments is June 4, 2026, 45 days from publication of this Notice. Members … will be permitted to phase in their implementation over a period of 18 months, until October 20, 2027."* SEC approval was Exchange Act Release No. 105226 (April 14, 2026), File No. SR-FINRA-2025-017. It is replaced by a real-time intraday margin framework; non-leveraged margin accounts have no minimum, and leveraged 4× buying power now needs only $2,000. **Alpaca confirmed** it removes all PDT restrictions on June 4, 2026 and will **delete the `pattern_day_trader`, `daytrade_count`, `daytrading_buying_power` API fields by July 6, 2026** — build sizing on `buying_power`. PDT historically applied only to margin accounts (not cash, not crypto). Alpaca **paper accounts simulate PDT logic**, so the same rules applied in the sandbox until the change.
- **Market hours:** Regular Trading Hours 9:30–16:00 ET; extended/overnight sessions exist but carry wider spreads and thinner liquidity. Generate signals on RTH bars; avoid the first ~1–2 minutes after the open (liquidity grabs) and be cautious around economic releases (CPI, FOMC, jobs) and earnings, which cause breakout-prone false moves.

### 5. Alpaca API (2026)
- **Endpoints:** paper REST `https://paper-api.alpaca.markets`, live REST `https://api.alpaca.markets`, market data `https://data.alpaca.markets`. `TradingClient(api_key, secret_key, paper=True)` selects the paper endpoint internally (paper uses a separate key/secret pair). Auth = API key ID + secret key headers.
- **SDK:** use **alpaca-py** (official; the older alpaca-trade-api is deprecated). OOP request objects: `MarketOrderRequest`, `LimitOrderRequest`, `StopOrderRequest`, `TrailingStopOrderRequest`, plus `TradingClient.submit_order/get_orders/get_all_positions/get_account`.
- **Market data:** `StockHistoricalDataClient` + `StockBarsRequest(symbol_or_symbols, timeframe, start, end, feed)`; `TimeFrame.Minute` for 1Min, `TimeFrame(5, TimeFrameUnit.Minute)` / `TimeFrame(15, ...)` for 5/15Min (REST strings `1Min`/`5Min`/`15Min`). **Feeds:** `DataFeed.IEX` (free, ~2% of total US market volume) vs `DataFeed.SIP` (100% of volume, requires Algo Trader Plus). Alpaca's Elite page confirms the price: *"Get Algo Trader Plus on us (value of $99/month)"*; the Basic free plan *"only includes limited real-time data: for equities only the IEX exchange,"* while Algo Trader Plus is *"directly fed by the CTA … and the UTP … encompassing 100% of market volume."* Historical SIP data older than 15 minutes is free. Real-time streaming via `StockDataStream` websocket (bars/trades/quotes); one websocket connection at a time.
- **Order types:** market, limit, stop, stop-limit, **bracket** (`OrderClass.BRACKET` + `TakeProfitRequest(limit_price=...)` + `StopLossRequest(stop_price=...)`), OTO, OCO, and trailing stop (`trail_price` or `trail_percent`, DAY/GTC only). A bracket is an OTOCO chain (entry + TP + SL; one exit cancels the other). **A trailing stop CANNOT yet be the stop-loss leg of a bracket** (Alpaca says "planned for the future") — manage trailing separately.
- **Rate limit:** **200 requests/minute per API key** on trading endpoints (429 on exceed); paid plans raise the *market-data* limit but trading stays at 200/min. Implement a client-side rate limiter and try/except with retry.
- **Critical data caveat (contrarian):** the free **IEX feed is a ~2% sample** — per Alpaca-integration docs (HawkAlpha), *"IEX market data only includes orders and executions on the IEX exchange, which account for about 2% of the total market,"* whereas Alpaca's SIP feed *"encompass[es] 100% of market volume."* Alpaca's own example shows AAPL on 2023-09-29 had 12,630 IEX trades vs 535,136+ on SIP. This **structurally undermines volume-confirmation breakout logic** (your key anti-fakeout filter) and can mis-set breakout levels. For volume-dependent intraday signals, either subscribe to SIP or lean on price-action confirmation and treat IEX volume as a rough proxy.

### 6. SQL Server schema design
Recommended tables:
- **watchlist** (`symbol PK`, `name`, `is_active`, `added_at`, optional sector/ATR cache).
- **trades** (`trade_id PK`, `symbol FK`, `side`, `entry_price`, `entry_time`, `qty`, `exit_price`, `exit_time`, `realized_pl`, `realized_pl_pct`, `status`, `alpaca_order_id`).
- **signals** (`signal_id PK`, `symbol`, `timestamp`, `signal_type` [breakout/MA/both], `confidence` 0–100, component sub-scores [breakout_score, ma_score, volume_score, momentum_score], `trade_id FK nullable`) — logs WHY each trade was taken.
- **daily_summary** (`date PK`, `symbol` or portfolio-level, `num_buys`, `num_sells`, `gross_pl`, `realized_pl_pct`, `win_count`, `loss_count`, `equity_close`).
- **Connectivity:** **pyodbc** is Microsoft's recommended, more feature-rich, widely-adopted driver (use `ODBC Driver 18 for SQL Server`); pymssql is a lighter cross-platform alternative; Microsoft's newer **mssql-python** is emerging. Always use **parameterized queries** (`?` placeholders) — never string-format SQL — to prevent injection. Connection string carries DRIVER/SERVER/DATABASE/UID/PWD or `Trusted_Connection=yes`. SSMS is the GUI for inspecting the data.

### 7. Secrets / API key storage (security)
- **Why plaintext-in-DB is dangerous:** anyone with read access to the DB (DBAs, backups, a SQL-injection hole, a leaked connection string, SSMS users) instantly obtains live trading + Telegram credentials; DB backups proliferate copies; there is no rotation/audit boundary. This violates separation of code/config from secrets.
- **Professional best practice:** keep keys OUT of the database. Use a **`.env` file loaded by python-dotenv** (`load_dotenv()` + `os.getenv()`), add `.env` to `.gitignore`, commit a `.env.example` template, and on the VPS set file permissions `chmod 600 .env`. Fail-fast if a required secret is missing. For higher assurance use the OS keychain or a secrets manager (AWS Secrets Manager / Azure Key Vault / HashiCorp Vault). Rotate keys periodically and use different keys per environment. Everything else (trades, signals, P&L, watchlist) still lives in SQL Server.

### 8. Telegram alerting
- **Setup:** message **@BotFather** → `/newbot` → receive bot token; get chat id by adding the bot to a chat and reading `https://api.telegram.org/bot<token>/getUpdates` (or via @userinfobot). Send via the **python-telegram-bot** library (`Bot(token).send_message(chat_id, text)`) or a plain `requests` POST to the Bot API `sendMessage`.
- **Good trade alert contains:** symbol, side, entry price, quantity/notional, confidence score + which signals fired, stop-loss & take-profit levels, timestamp, and account equity/P&L context. Also send error/heartbeat alerts and a daily P&L summary. Store token + chat id in `.env`, not the DB.

### 9. Architecture & phased build
- **Layers:** (1) data ingestion (poll/stream Alpaca bars), (2) signal/strategy engine (S/R + triple-EMA + filters), (3) confidence + risk/position sizing, (4) order execution (bracket orders), (5) SQL Server logging, (6) Telegram alerting, (7) scheduler/main loop respecting market hours.
- **Deployment:** a small Linux VPS (e.g., Ubuntu 22.04, 2GB RAM, ~$6–10/mo, NY region for proximity to US brokers); run as a long-lived process under **systemd** with `Restart=on-failure` and `EnvironmentFile=` pointing to the 600-perms `.env` (do NOT put secrets in `Environment=`). Alternatives: supervisor, pm2, or screen/tmux for quick tests. Add UptimeRobot/heartbeat monitoring, structured logging, SSH key auth, and a UFW firewall (deny inbound). Set a hard max position size in code.
- **Recommended phase order (Claude Code-friendly):** Phase 1 env + DB schema + Alpaca paper connection + secrets; Phase 2 data ingestion; Phase 3 S/R detection; Phase 4 triple-EMA signals + regime filter; Phase 5 confidence scoring + ATR position sizing; Phase 6 order execution (bracket) on paper; Phase 7 DB logging + daily summary; Phase 8 Telegram alerts; Phase 9 scheduler/main loop with market-hours guard; Phase 10 VPS deployment + monitoring; then a long paper-incubation phase before any live capital.

## Details

### Reality checks the plan must encode
- **ORB / breakout edge is real but fragile.** The strongest evidence is Zarattini & Aziz, *"Can Day Trading Really Be Profitable?"* (SSRN 4416622, 2023): the ORB portfolio *"produced an annualized alpha of 33% (net of commissions)"* — a 1,484% total return 2016–2023 vs QQQ's 169%. The follow-up Zarattini, Barbon & Aziz, *"A Profitable Day Trading Strategy For The U.S. Equity Market"* (SSRN 4729284, 2024) reports a top-20 "Stocks-in-Play" portfolio with *"a Sharpe ratio of 2.81, and an annualized alpha of 36%"* (>1,600% total return). **But these depend on leverage, shorting "Stocks in Play," and zero-slippage assumptions** that retail Alpaca execution cannot reproduce. False-breakout rates are commonly cited at 40–70%, and the edge appears to decay when you can't isolate the right names: QuantifiedStrategies.com (which notes it has *"NOT backtested or verified the results"*) finds that trading all eligible stocks rather than just Stocks in Play yields *"a Sharpe Ratio of 0.48, significantly lower than the S&P 500's 0.78,"* because *"the ORB strategy relies on identifying an abnormal imbalance … Trading all eligible stocks fails to isolate days where this abnormality is strong enough to overcome transaction costs."* Build with confirmation filters and modest expectations.
- **Paper trading overstates performance.** Alpaca paper fills are simulated against the (IEX) quote with no slippage, no queue position, no market impact, and no psychology. Slippage is worst exactly on fast breakout moves where your best signals occur, and it is correlated with your best trades — so average slippage understates the damage. Log paper vs (eventual) live to quantify.
- **Overfitting is the silent killer.** A breakout + triple-MA + weighted-confidence + ATR-sizing system has many free parameters. Guardrails: reserve ≥30% out-of-sample data (never tune on it), use walk-forward analysis, prefer parameter *plateaus* over single optima, minimize tuned weights, and consider the Deflated Sharpe Ratio / Probability of Backtest Overfitting. The academic base rate is sobering: per Barber, Lee, Liu & Odean, *"The Cross-Section of Speculator Skill: Evidence from Day Trading,"* Journal of Financial Markets 18 (2014) 1–24 — analyzing Taiwan day traders 1992–2006 — *"Less than 1% of the day trader population is able to predictably and reliably earn positive abnormal returns net of fees"* (the top-500 traders earn +37.9 bps/day after fees, while the bottom earn −28.9 bps/day).

## Recommendations
1. **Phase 1–2 first, prove the plumbing:** stand up the SQL Server schema, `.env` secrets, Alpaca paper connection (`paper=True`), and reliable bar ingestion before any strategy code. Benchmark: bot can fetch 1/5/15-min bars for the watchlist, write to DB, and survive a restart via systemd.
2. **Build signals with confirmation, not raw crossovers:** require candle-close breakouts + a regime filter (ADX>20 or higher-timeframe EMA slope) before triple-EMA alignment counts. Threshold to change course: if paper win-rate < ~45% or false-breakout rate > 40%, tighten volume/buffer filters.
3. **Size defensively from day one:** hard cap 1–2% risk per trade, quarter-to-half Kelly at most, ATR stops at 0.6–1.0× intraday, bracket orders so every entry has a server-side stop. Benchmark before scaling: 50–100+ paper trades with stable expectancy.
4. **Fix the data feed decision early:** if volume-confirmed breakouts are core, budget for Alpaca SIP ($99/mo Algo Trader Plus) or explicitly downweight IEX volume and rely on price action. Don't trust IEX volume spikes.
5. **Don't go live until incubated:** run paper for at least several weeks–months, compare to expectations, and only then deploy small live capital. Re-evaluate sizing/filters monthly.
6. **Security is non-negotiable:** keys in `.env` (gitignored, chmod 600), never in SQL Server or source; rotate periodically; `EnvironmentFile=` in systemd.

## Caveats
- The PDT elimination (June 4, 2026) and Alpaca's PDT-field removal (by July 6, 2026) are very recent; verify current Alpaca account/margin behavior at build time and base logic on `buying_power`.
- Specific breakout win-rate percentages (52–53% ORB, 60–70% fakeout) come largely from vendor blogs citing unnamed studies — treat as directional, not authoritative. The Zarattini/Aziz research is credible but co-authored by a day-trading educator (Andrew Aziz, Bear Bull Traders) and rests on idealized execution.
- ATR multipliers, MA periods, confidence weights, and risk % are starting points requiring out-of-sample validation on your specific universe; they are not guarantees.
- Alpaca's Algo Trader Plus $99/month figure is stated on Alpaca's Elite page and corroborated secondarily, not printed inline on the market-data docs page — confirm current pricing.
- This is an engineering/educational design, not investment advice; day trading carries substantial risk of loss.
